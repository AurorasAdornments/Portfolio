Best movies according to Candy

Below is a list of my top 3 movies, a short review and rating of all of them

hereditary

"Hereditary" is a chilling psychological horror film that explores family dynamics and the emotional impact of supernatural events.

Exceptional performances, slow-burning tension, and clever symbolism make it a standout in the genre.

Director Ari Aster's direction adds to the disorienting and terrifying atmosphere, while the film's lingering sense of dread sets it apart from jump-scare-heavy counterparts.

If you're a fan of psychological horror, "Hereditary" is a must-see that will stay with you long after it ends.

Django unchained

"Django Unchained" is a gripping and thought-provoking film that skillfully blends Quentin Tarantino's signature style with a powerful examination of America's history of slavery.

Foxx delivers a compelling performance as the titular character, a freed slave on a quest for justice.

The film's sharp writing, exceptional cast, and intense action sequences keep you engaged throughout.

Tarantino's unique storytelling approach combines humor, brutality, and social commentary in a way that's both entertaining and thought-provoking.

"Django Unchained" is a standout addition to the director's filmography and a must-watch for those who appreciate bold, genre-defying cinema.

Coraline

"Coraline" is a visually captivating animated film with a unique blend of whimsy and darkness.

Directed by Henry Selick, it tells the story of a young girl's discovery of a hidden world, blending enchantment and unease.

The meticulous stop-motion animation and exploration of themes like bravery and escapism make it a must-see.

The film's hauntingly beautiful soundtrack and superb voice acting enhance its dark and imaginative journey, leaving a lasting impression.
